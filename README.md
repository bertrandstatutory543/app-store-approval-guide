## 💳 Payments and Subscriptions

Apple checks payment flows closely. This guide helps you review:

- Trial wording
- Subscription length
- Renewal text
- Cancelation text
- Price labels
- Restore purchase flow
- Buy button text
- Free vs paid feature labels

Use simple words in your app. For example, say Monthly Plan, Yearly Plan, or Restore Purchase. Avoid vague labels that make users guess what they will get.

If your app includes a paid feature, make sure the user sees that before they tap it. This reduces the chance of review issues.

## 🔒 Legal and Policy Checks

This app also helps you check legal details that Apple expects to see. Review these items:

- Privacy policy link works
- Terms of use are easy to reach
- Data collection is explained
- Account deletion steps are clear
- Age rating fits your content
- Third-party content is disclosed
- Health, finance, or location features are explained
- User-generated content has clear rules

If your app handles personal data, explain why you collect it. Keep the wording short and direct. The reviewer should not need to search for the answer.

## 👁️ Transparency Checklist

A clear app earns trust faster. Check that your app shows:

- What the app does
- What users can do for free
- What requires payment
- What data the app uses
- What permissions the app needs
- What happens after sign-up
- How users can get help

If your app asks for camera, photos, contacts, or location access, explain the reason before the prompt appears. That helps users understand the request.

## 🧪 Review Prep Tips

Use these steps before you submit your app:

- Test every button that leads to payment
- Open every link you plan to include in review notes
- Check the app on a fresh test account
- Remove placeholder text
- Make sure no fake data appears in the final build
- Confirm that the app loads without broken screens
- Verify that support email and policy links work

A clean final build gives the reviewer less to question.
